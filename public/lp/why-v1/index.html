Why we built Spontany.

Short version: I got divorced, spent years staring at calendars, and realized the hardest part wasn't custody — it was coordinating a life around it. So I built the thing I wished existed.

Who it's for

Divorced parents with kids aged 0–14. You've got a custody schedule, and half your mental energy goes to texting "do you have the kids that weekend?" Spontany does that math once, quietly, and lets you both see the overlap.

Why it's free

No ads. No selling data. No freemium gates. At least not yet.

The real answer: I'd rather 5,000 people use it and make one compound suggestion than 50 people pay $9/month for a half-built product. If it works, I'll figure out pricing later — probably a small fee to unlock friend-group planning or venue recommendations. The core custody sync will stay free. Always.

How your privacy works

  • Your schedule is visible only to people you explicitly invite.
  • You can revoke access any time — it's one tap.
  • We don't share, sell, or look at your data to show you ads.
  • If you delete your account, we delete your data. For real.

What it won't do

Spontany won't fix your coparent relationship, won't solve your custody dispute, won't replace therapy. It's a calendar overlap tool. A really good one — and one that's built for the shape your life actually has.
