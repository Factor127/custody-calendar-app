# Spontany Landing Page — Stitch Brief

---

## Prompt for Stitch

Design a dark-themed, modern landing page for **Spontany** — a scheduling app for divorcees that helps them coordinate plans around custody schedules with partners and friend groups.

### Brand

- **Tagline:** "Know before you ask."
- **Secondary line:** "Finds your moments before they slip away."
- **Voice:** Smart, fun, mature, sexy. Think confident grown-up energy — not clinical, not cutesy.
- **Audience:** Divorcees aged 35–55 with kids. They're dating again, making plans with friends, and tired of the scheduling chaos.

### Color Palette (strict)

Inspired by a dark + olive/lime green editorial aesthetic (see reference image below). High contrast, bold, modern.

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Background | `#0a0a0a` | Primary page background — true dark, near-black |
| Surface | `#141414` | Cards, sections, elevated elements |
| Lime/Olive (accent) | `#c4d630` | Primary accent — CTAs, highlights, section backgrounds, energy. This is THE color. |
| Lime (dark) | `#a3b325` | Hover states, secondary accent, tinted surfaces |
| White | `#ffffff` | Headlines, primary body text on dark |
| Dark text | `#1a1a1a` | Text on lime/olive backgrounds |
| Muted | `#888888` | Secondary/supporting text on dark |

**Note:** The app itself uses purple (#7c5cbf) and orange (#f97316) — but for the landing page, lean into this lime/olive + dark palette for a bolder, more editorial first impression. The app colors appear naturally when showing UI mockups.

### Typography

Clean sans-serif. Inter, SF Pro, or similar. High contrast white on dark. Headlines should feel bold and confident, body copy should feel light and breathable.

### Design Style

**Long-scroll editorial — dark, cinematic, story-driven.** A single-column vertical journey that reveals the product section by section. Each section is its own moment. Inspired by two references:

1. **Color reference:** "Plan. Trigger. Relax." — dark background + lime/olive green accent for CTAs, highlights, and tinted areas. Oversized stat numbers. Pill-shaped buttons.
2. **Layout reference:** "Meet Play." app landing page — long single-column scroll, centered content, big bold headlines, phone/device mockups between sections, generous dark space between every element, bottom CTA for beta signup. Minimal, cinematic, story-like progression.

Key style notes:
- True dark background (#0a0a0a) — near-black, consistent throughout the entire page
- Lime/olive green (#c4d630) as the accent — CTAs, highlights, tinted mockup areas
- **Single-column centered layout** — no side-by-side hero splits. Content flows vertically.
- Big, bold headlines centered or left-aligned — each section starts with a punchy headline
- **Phone mockups showing the Spontany calendar UI** between sections — the app is a PWA, so show it in a phone frame to feel native
- Massive vertical spacing between sections — let the dark breathe. Each section should feel like a scene, not a row.
- Pill-shaped CTA buttons: lime/olive background, dark text
- No stock photos. Use phone mockups, abstract shapes, or stylized UI screenshots.
- Stats section with oversized numbers in lime/olive
- Bottom section: "Join the beta" style CTA with email signup
- Mobile responsive — the single-column layout naturally works on mobile

### Page Structure

**Section 1 — Hero (carousel)**
- Layout: Centered, vertical. Rotating scenario line at top, fixed massive headline, subline, CTA, phone mockup.
- **Rotating scenario line** (cycles every 3–4 seconds, smooth crossfade):
  1. *"Not sure when she's got the kids?"*
  2. *"6 friends. 6 custody schedules. 0 plans."*
  3. *"Will you be with your kids or can we do this?"*
  4. *"Found the perfect Saturday night gig?"*
  5. *"2 people planning. 4 homes aligning."*
- **Fixed headline** (massive, bold, centered, stacked): **"Know\nbefore\nyou ask."**
- **Fixed subline:** "Spontany maps custody schedules so plans happen in seconds, not days."
- **CTA button:** **"Get early access →"** — pill-shaped, lime/olive background, dark text
- Below CTA: A phone mockup showing the Spontany dark calendar UI — custody days marked, an overlap moment highlighted. The phone floats on the dark background, cinematic.
- The rotating line creates a "every angle hits home" effect — each visitor sees themselves in at least one scenario.

**Section 2 — Features (2 rows of 3 blocks)**

Layout: Two rows of 3 cards/blocks on dark surface. Each block has: icon or small visual, bold headline, 2-line description. Clean grid, generous spacing.

**Row 1:**

| Block | Headline | Copy |
|-------|----------|------|
| **2 custodies overlap view** | "See months ahead. Plan without asking." | Your custody schedule and theirs — overlaid in one view. See when you're both free tonight, next week, or in July. No awkward questions. Just open the app and plan. |
| **Group availability** | "One screen. Everyone's free days." | 6 friends. 6 custody schedules. Finding one night used to be impossible. Spontany shows the group overlap instantly — so you stop polling and start planning. |
| **Link to "I'm in"** | "Drop a link. See who's free. Invite." | Paste an event URL — Spontany pulls the date, time, and location automatically. It shows which friends are available that day. One tap to invite. Seconds, not a 12-message thread. |

**Row 2:**

| Block | Headline | Copy |
|-------|----------|------|
| **Co-parent sync** | "Coordinate without the conversation." | Pickup changes. Schedule swaps. Weekend updates. All the logistics that currently live in a text thread you'd rather not open. It's all in the app now. |
| **Export to kids** | "They know the plan too." | Share the schedule with your kids so everyone knows who's where and when. Less confusion, fewer "wait, am I at mum's or dad's?" moments. |
| **Visibility control** | "Who sees what. And for how long." | Grant access to your calendar on your terms. Friends see availability. Partners see the full picture. Set time-limited access that expires when you want it to. Every connection is invite-only. |

**Section 3 — Stats**
- Oversized numbers with short labels:
  - **"0"** — "Awkward 'do you have the kids?' texts"
  - **"2 min"** — "To set up your calendar"
  - **"1 tap"** — "From event link to invite"
  - **"4→1"** — "Homes aligned, one screen"
- Big bold numbers in lime/olive, labels in white or muted grey.

**Section 4 — Social Proof / Relatable Moment**
- A single relatable quote in large italic text, centered:
  *"He just opened the app, saw I was free Thursday, and texted 'Italian?' One message. Done."*
- Below: avatar + name + context (e.g., "— Early beta user")
- Lots of dark space around it. Let the quote breathe.

**Section 5 — FAQ**
- Collapsible accordion style on dark surface. Clean, minimal.

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "Who can see my custody schedule?" | You control everything. Friends see when you're free — not your custody details. Partners see the shared calendar. Co-parents see what they need for coordination. No one sees more than they should. Every connection is invite-only, and you can set access to expire whenever you want. |
| "Is this a co-parenting app?" | No. Spontany is a scheduling and lifestyle app. It's built for people whose lives involve custody schedules — but the point is making plans, not managing co-parenting. |
| "Do I need to download anything?" | Nope. Spontany is a Progressive Web App — it runs in your browser and you can add it to your home screen like a native app. Works on any device. |
| "Is it free?" | Yes. Spontany is free to use. |
| "What if my ex doesn't want to use it?" | No problem. You don't need your ex on Spontany. Add your custody schedule yourself, invite the people you actually want to plan with — partner, friends — and go. |

**Section 6 — Bottom CTA (beta signup)**
- Large bold stacked headline: **"Join Our\nEarly Access."**
- Short description: "Spontany is free, works on any device, and takes 2 minutes to set up. No app store needed."
- Email input field + **"Get early access →"** pill button in lime/olive
- Small muted text: "We'll let you know when it's your turn."

**Footer**
- Spontany logo (small, centered)
- Links: Privacy Policy · Terms of Service
- "Made for the moments that matter."
- Minimal. Just a few lines on dark.

### What NOT to do

- No bright/white backgrounds (olive/lime sections are OK — that's the accent, not "bright")
- No purple or orange as primary landing page colors (save those for in-app)
- No stock photography
- No hearts, cupid imagery, or dating app clichés
- No childish illustrations or cartoon characters
- Don't make it look like a family app or a co-parenting tool — it's a lifestyle/scheduling app
- Don't use "divorced" prominently in the hero — the audience knows who they are
