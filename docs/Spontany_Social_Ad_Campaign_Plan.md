# Spontany Social Ad Campaign Plan

**Campaign Name:** "Know Before You Ask"
**Date:** March 30, 2026
**Duration:** 2 weeks (April 1–14, 2026)
**Budget:** Under $1,000
**Platforms:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram), TikTok, X

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Summary:** A two-track paid social campaign introducing Spontany — the scheduling app that lets divorcees see when friends and dates are free, without the awkward ask — to single parents navigating custody-driven scheduling chaos.

**Primary Objective:** Build a pre-launch waitlist — target 500+ email signups across all platforms in 2 weeks.

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Build brand awareness within the divorced-parent community
- Validate which ad concept (dating vs. friend group) resonates more strongly
- Establish Spontany's brand tone: smart, fun, mature, sexy

---

## 2. Target Audience

**Primary Segment: "The Newly Navigating Dad"**
- Men, 35–55, divorced or separated, with kids aged 0–14
- Shares custody (50/50, weekends, or alternating weeks)
- Actively dating or wanting to date, but frustrated by scheduling complexity
- Pain points: doesn't know when she's free, feels awkward asking "do you have your kids this weekend?", tired of back-and-forth texting to lock down plans
- Spends time on: Instagram Reels, TikTok, X, dating apps, dad-life subreddits, Facebook groups for single parents

**Secondary Segment: "The Social Divorced Friend Group"**
- Men and women, 35–55, divorced, trying to maintain active social lives
- Friend groups where everyone has different custody schedules
- Pain points: group plans fall apart because nobody knows who's free when, planning a night out takes 47 messages
- Spends time on: Instagram Stories, Facebook groups, WhatsApp/group chats

**Buying Stage:** Awareness → Consideration (cold audience, introducing a new product category)

---

## 3. Key Messages

**Core Campaign Message:** "Know before you ask."

### Ad Set A — Dating Angle
**Hook:** "Not sure when she's got the kids?"
**Payoff:** "Know before you ask. Get her on Spontany."
**Supporting messages:**
- Stop guessing. See when she's actually free.
- No more awkward "so… do you have the kids this weekend?" texts.
- She'll thank you for not being the 5th guy to ask that question today.

**Proof points:** Spontany syncs custody schedules so availability is visible without anyone having to explain their situation.

### Ad Set B — Friend Group Angle
**Hook:** "Having a hard time finding a time that works for everyone?"
**Payoff:** "Know before you ask."
**Supporting messages:**
- 6 friends. 6 custody schedules. 1 app that actually figures it out.
- Stop waiting for everyone to "check and get back to you."
- Your group chat has 200 messages and still no plan. There's a better way.

**Proof points:** Spontany shows overlapping free windows across your entire friend group instantly.

---

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Meta (Facebook + Instagram) — Primary Channel
**Why:** Strongest targeting for divorced parents 35–55. Facebook groups are a natural gathering point for this community. Instagram Reels for punchy, visual storytelling.
**Formats:**
- Instagram Reels (15–30s vertical video or motion graphic)
- Facebook Feed ads (single image + copy, carousel)
- Instagram Stories (swipe-up/link sticker)
**Budget allocation:** $500 (50%)
**Effort:** High (most creative variants here)

### TikTok — Secondary Channel
**Why:** Growing 35+ user base. Relatable humor and "real talk" content performs well. Good for the dating angle — divorce TikTok is massive.
**Formats:**
- In-feed ads (15–30s vertical video, native/UGC style)
- Spark Ads (if organic content is created first)
**Budget allocation:** $300 (30%)
**Effort:** Medium

### X (Twitter) — Tertiary / Test Channel
**Why:** Good for witty, text-forward creative that matches Spontany's smart/funny tone. Lower CPMs. Strong for cultural conversation.
**Formats:**
- Promoted tweets (text + image or short video)
- Thread-style ads
**Budget allocation:** $200 (20%)
**Effort:** Low

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: Cold audience, new app — low trust/conversion.**
Mitigation: Lead with relatable pain points, not features. "Know before you ask" sells the feeling, not the product. Ensure App Store listing is polished with social proof if available.

**Risk 2: Dating angle could feel creepy if tone is off.**
Mitigation: Keep it playful and empowering, never surveillance-y. The message is "she shares her schedule with you" not "you can track when she has her kids." Test copy with a few people before launch.

**Risk 3: $1K budget spread across 3 platforms may be too thin for signal.**
Mitigation: Be aggressive about cutting underperformers by Day 3. Be willing to go all-in on 1 platform if one clearly outperforms.
