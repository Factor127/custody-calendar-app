**Version:** 1.0
**Created:** 2026-05-10
**Owner:** Ran
**Status:** Canonical. This is the source of truth for all Spontany user interviews. Future digital forms (Tally, Typeform, Google Forms) should be generated from this document.

## What this guide is for

A repeatable, behavior-first interview script for understanding how a real person experiences (or fails to experience) Spontany. The goal of every interview is the same: find the specific moment of value, in the interviewee's own language, so we can recreate that moment for strangers in the wedge and the LP.

This guide works for four interviewee types. The skeleton is the same; a few sections adapt:

| Type | Who | What changes |
|------|-----|-------------|
| **Active user** | Has used the app more than once | Section 4 (what they actually use) is the heart |
| **Lapsed user** | Signed up, never returned | Section 3 (first moment) and Section 5 (friction) are the heart |
| **Prospect** | Fits the persona, has not used the app | Skip Sections 3–6, focus on Sections 1–2 + Section 9 |
| **Power user** | High-frequency user / partner-style | Section 7 (sharing) is the heart |

For each interview, fill in the **Pre-Interview Setup** at the top of the response file before starting.

## Interviewer rules (read before every interview)

1. **Behavior before opinion.** Ask what they did, not what they think. "Walk me through the last time you…" beats "Do you usually…"
2. **Don't pitch the product.** Don't explain features. Don't correct misunderstandings of how the app works — what they think it does *is* what it does, for them.
3. **Don't defend.** If they criticize something you built, write it down and ask a follow-up. Don't argue. Don't fix it mid-call.
4. **Ask "why" five times.** Push past the first plausible answer. Most truth lives two layers deeper than people will volunteer.
5. **Take notes after, not during.** Notes during the call distract you from the face. Record audio with permission, write notes within 24 hours.
6. **Sit somewhere casual.** Not a desk. Coffee, couch, walk — whatever lowers the formality. Phone for recording is fine.
7. **Watch the face.** Words can lie. The look on someone's face when you ask "what would you miss if I shut it down tomorrow" can't.

## Section 1 — Setup (3 min)

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q1 | WARMUP | How's your week been? |
| Q2 | TIMELINE | Roughly when did you start using Spontany — do you remember? |
| Q3 | TRIGGER | Was there a specific moment that made you start, or was it gradual? |

*Notes for interviewer:* Q1 is throwaway, ignore the answer. Use Q2/Q3 to anchor everything that follows in real time.

---

## Section 2 — Replay the last use (10 min)

**This is the most important section.** Push for specifics. Don't accept abstractions.

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q4 | REPLAY | Walk me through the last time you opened Spontany. What were you doing right before? |
| Q5 | REPLAY | What did you do first when you opened it? |
| Q6 | INTENT | What were you actually trying to figure out? |
| Q7 | OBSERVATION | What did you see on the screen — describe it like I've never seen it. |
| Q8 | OUTCOME | Did you find what you were looking for? How did you know? |
| Q9 | AFTER | What did you do after you closed the app? |

*Probes:* If they say "I just wanted to see when we're free" — push: *"Free for what? Was there something specific you were thinking about?"*

---

## Section 3 — The first moment (8 min)

Hunting for when it clicked.

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q10 | MAGIC | Do you remember the first time you opened it and thought "oh, this is actually useful"? |
| Q11 | OBSERVATION | What were you looking at on the screen at that moment? |
| Q12 | SURPRISE | Was there anything you saw that surprised you — that you didn't expect? |
| Q13 | COUNTERFACTUAL | Before Spontany, how were you doing this? |
| Q14 | COMPARISON | What did the old way feel like, compared to now? |

*Notes for interviewer:* If they struggle to remember a specific aha moment — that's data. It might mean the click was gradual, not a single moment. Mark it as "no discrete aha".

---

## Section 4 — What they actually use (5 min)

Test each feature with a behavior question, not an opinion question. Adapt the feature list per interviewee.

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q15 | BEHAVIOR | When was the last time you used [feature X]? What were you doing? |
| Q16 | BEHAVIOR | The [feature Y] — when did you first notice it? What did you do with that information? |
| Q17 | MOMENT | When you say you like [thing they mentioned] — tell me about a specific time that mattered. What were you about to do, what did the app show you, what did you do next? |

*The trap:* They'll be tempted to give positive opinions out of politeness. *"That's nice"* is not data. *"Last Tuesday I was about to tell my mom I couldn't, then I checked and saw…"* is data. Push for the second kind.

---

## Section 5 — Friction & avoidance (5 min)

What they don't say is as important as what they do.

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q18 | NEGATIVE | Is there anything in the app you've tried and stopped using? |
| Q19 | AVOIDANCE | Anything you avoid clicking on? |
| Q20 | FRUSTRATION | Has there been a moment where you almost gave up or were annoyed? |
| Q21 | EXPECTATION | Anything you expected it to do that it doesn't? |
| Q22 | TEACHING | If you had to teach a friend to use it, where would you worry they'd get stuck? |

---

## Section 6 — The counterfactual (5 min)

The cleanest test of real value.

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q23 | COUNTERFACTUAL | If I deleted it from your phone tomorrow, what specifically would you miss? |
| Q24 | COUNTERFACTUAL | Would you go back to the old way, or would you find a different solution? |
| Q25 | EMOTIONAL | What would you say to me if I told you I was shutting it down? |

*Watch the face on Q25.* Words can lie, faces can't.

---

## Section 7 — Sharing & word-of-mouth (5 min)

The most strategically important section if the interviewee is an active user. We need to understand why people aren't sharing organically.

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q26 | BEHAVIOR | Have you ever thought about telling someone else about it? |
| Q27 | STORY | If yes — who, what stopped you, or what happened? |
| Q28 | BARRIER | If no — why not? What would have to be true for you to want to? |
| Q29 | LANGUAGE | Imagine your closest [persona-fit] friend asks "what's that app you use?" — what do you actually say? |
| Q30 | WEDGE | What would they need to see in the first 30 seconds for it to click for them the way it did for you? |

**Q30 is the wedge headline candidate.** Whatever answer they give is what the LP needs to deliver.

---

## Section 8 — Open close (3 min)

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| Q31 | OPEN | If you could change one thing about it, what would it be? |
| Q32 | OPEN | Anything else you've been wanting to tell me about it? |

## Section 9 — Prospect-only adapter (use INSTEAD of 2–6 if interviewee has never used the app)

| ID | Type | Question |
|----|------|----------|
| QP1 | PAIN | Tell me about the last time you tried to plan something with someone and it didn't happen. What got in the way? |
| QP2 | WORKAROUND | How do you handle that today? |
| QP3 | EMOTION | When that breaks down, what's the actual feeling — annoyance, resignation, something else? |
| QP4 | DEMO | (Show the wedge.) Walk me through what you think this is asking you to do. Don't try to be right — just narrate what you see. |
| QP5 | REACTION | When you got to the result page, what was your first reaction? |
| QP6 | INTENT | If this worked the way it seems to, would you actually use it? Why or why not? |

## Post-Interview Synthesis (within 24 hours)

For every interview, capture three things in writing. These are what get reused; the raw transcript is archive material.

1. **The single most specific moment of value the interviewee described** — quoted, with context.
2. **What they said they'd miss if it were gone** — and whether that matches what we thought the value was.
3. **What they'd say to a friend in 30 seconds — verbatim.** This is the wedge headline candidate.

Add the synthesis to `strategy/interviews/<interview-id>.md` along with the audio file and a link to the recording.

## Digitization notes (for whoever turns this into a form)

Each question has a stable ID (`Q1`–`Q32`, `QP1`–`QP6`) and a type tag. When digitizing:

- **REPLAY / BEHAVIOR / STORY / MOMENT** questions need free-text fields with no character limit — these are the qualitative gold.
- **TIMELINE / COUNTERFACTUAL** questions can be short text or single-line answers.
- **WARMUP / OPEN** questions are optional and can be skipped if time is tight.
- Section 4 (Q15–Q17) is templated — the interviewer must fill in the feature names per session, so the digital form needs editable per-interview fields.
- Section 9 is the prospect adapter — the form should branch based on the interviewee type selected in Pre-Interview Setup.

The recommended digital format is a hybrid: a structured form for the answers, plus a single audio file uploaded per interview. Don't transcribe live — transcription happens after.
